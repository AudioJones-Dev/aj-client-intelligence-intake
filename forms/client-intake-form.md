# Master Client Intake Form

## Purpose

Capture enough business context to generate a client-specific knowledge vault, CRM model, AI receptionist setup, and ResponseOS deployment brief.

## Section 1 — Company Profile

1. Legal business name
2. Public business name
3. Website
4. Primary phone number
5. Primary email
6. Physical address
7. Service area
8. Years in business
9. Owner / primary decision-maker
10. Key team members

## Section 2 — Business Model

1. What does the company sell or provide?
2. What are the main services?
3. What services are highest value?
4. What services are most urgent?
5. What services should the AI never promise without human confirmation?
6. Who are the best customers?
7. Who are poor-fit customers?

## Section 3 — Customers and Segments

1. Residential customers
2. Commercial customers
3. Contractors/builders
4. Property managers
5. Government or institutional clients
6. Repeat clients
7. Referral partners

## Section 4 — Sales Process

1. How do leads usually arrive?
2. What qualifies a good lead?
3. What makes a lead urgent?
4. What information must be collected before an estimate?
5. Who follows up?
6. What are common objections?
7. What should the AI say when pricing is requested?

## Section 5 — Scheduling and Operations

1. How are appointments scheduled?
2. What information is required before dispatch?
3. What jobs require photos?
4. What jobs require site measurements?
5. What jobs require permits or compliance review?
6. What jobs should be escalated immediately?

## Section 6 — AI Receptionist Rules

1. What should the AI receptionist be allowed to answer?
2. What should the AI receptionist never answer?
3. When should the AI transfer or escalate?
4. Should the AI book appointments directly or collect lead details only?
5. What tone should the AI use?
6. What emergency scenarios matter?
7. What disclaimers are required?

## Section 7 — CRM

1. Current CRM, if any
2. Lead stages
3. Customer fields
4. Deal fields
5. Required notes
6. Follow-up rules
7. Lost lead reasons
8. Source tracking requirements

## Section 8 — Documents and Uploads

Request upload or collection of:

- Service list
- Pricing sheets
- FAQs
- Contracts
- Customer intake forms
- Existing SOPs
- Product manuals
- Warranty documents
- Team roster
- Call scripts
- Brand assets

## Section 9 — Open Questions

1. What is not documented but lives in the owner's head?
2. What mistakes should the AI avoid?
3. What customer situations often create confusion?
4. What rules are different for specific customers or vendors?
5. What needs human approval before going live?
